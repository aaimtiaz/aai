Investigating the relation between the environment and internal structure of massive elliptical galaxies using strong lensing

Supervisor: Dr. Anowar J Shajib; A&A, July 2025

This project explores how large-scale environments influence the internal structure of massive elliptical galaxies. By analyzing a sample of galaxy-galaxy strong lenses, we characterized the line-of-sight environment, and analyzing results to identify correlations between centroid offsets, position angle offsets, and the density of galaxies in the lens galaxyâ€™s environment. We robustly find that the centroid offset between the mass and light is not correlated with the local galaxy density and we found a moderate to strong correlation between the position angle offset and the standard definition of the local galaxy density.

Detailed study of chemical composition of lensed quiescent galaxy AGEL0014

Supervisor: Dr. Nicha Leethochawalit (Ongoing)

In this project, I am analyzing the chemical composition of the lens galaxy AGEL0014 using imaging data from HST and Gemini/GSAOI and spectroscopic data from Keck/MOSFIRE. After successfully modeling AGEL0014, I used the ray-shooting method in Lenstronomy to map image-plane segments into the source plane for metallicity gradient analysis. Currently, I am combining the spectra with the outputs from lens modeling to obtain a metallicity and age gradient of this lensed z~1 quiescent galaxy.

The study of stellar population of Sparkler galaxy

Supervisor: Dr. Lamiya Mowla (Ongoing)

I recently began a research internship under Dr. Lamiya Mowla through a collaborative program with ICTP for Bangladeshi students. In this project, I am working on the Sparkler galaxy, where my role involves modeling the source-plane image to study the properties of its stellar population.

Investigation of the Hubble constant and residual using optical and near-infrared observations of Supernova

Supervisor: Dr. Sayed Ashraf Uddin (Ongoing)

Recently, I initiated a project with Dr. Sayed Ashraf Uddin focusing on supernova cosmology. Using data from the Carnegie Supernova Project (CSP), I will investigate the Hubble residual as a function of host galaxy properties and address the ongoing Hubble constant tension by comparing optical and near-infrared observations (uBgVriYJH) of supernovae.
